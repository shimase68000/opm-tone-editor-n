## Tone Memo

The tone memo feature was designed not only as a place to describe a tone,\
but also as a space to capture thoughts during editing.

While editing a tone, users often have ideas, observations, or intentions\
that are worth recording immediately.

In many tools, such memo features tend to become secondary:

* users forget that memo exists
* accessing it requires explicit action
* it is treated as an optional or hidden feature

As a result, the memo is not normally used effectively.

In OED / OPM Tone Editor, the tone memo is not treated as a secondary or optional feature.

In Tone Editor, the memo is handled differently:

> the tone memo is frequently displayed and can be edited at any time

When a tone is selected from the tone list,\
its tone name and memo are automatically displayed.

Higher-priority messages (such as file save/load notifications)\
may temporarily override the display,\
but normal user actions (such as switching parameter tables)\
re-display the memo.

Although there may be brief moments when the memo is not visible,\
it can always be re-displayed or edited via the EXEC menu.

This allows users to naturally refer to the memo during tone editing.

The memo is intended to capture thoughts such as the following,\
with the aim of becoming a natural part of the editing process:

* what this tone was intended to become
* "almost there, but saving at this stage for now"
* "changing this parameter led to a new discovery"
* "envelope adjusted based on another tone"

with the aim of becoming a natural part of the editing process.
